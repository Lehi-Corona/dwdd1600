Who were the Signers

Famous signers

Thomas Jefferson
The primary author of the Declaration, Jefferson later became the third U.S. President and played a major role in shaping the nation's early policies, including the Louisiana Purchase.
John Adams
A strong advocate for independence, Adams later became the second U.S. President and was instrumental in negotiating peace with Britain after the war.
Benjamin Franklin
A polymath and diplomat, Franklin helped secure French support during the Revolutionary War and contributed significantly to the U.S. Constitution.
John Hancock
Known for his bold signature, Hancock was the president of the Continental Congress and a prominent merchant and patriot leader.
Samuel Adams
A key organizer of resistance against British rule, Adams helped orchestrate events like the Boston Tea Party and later became Governor of Massachusetts.
Richard Henry Lee
Introduced the resolution for independence in the Continental Congress and was a strong advocate for states' rights.
Roger Sherman
A skilled legislator, Sherman contributed to the drafting of the U.S. Constitution and was the only person to sign all major founding documents.
Charles Carroll of Carrollton
The only Catholic signer, Carroll was a wealthy landowner and a strong advocate for religious freedom.
Robert Morris
Known as the "Financier of the Revolution," Morris played a critical role in funding the war and establishing the nation's financial system.

Each of these individuals played a significant role in the founding of the United States of America and is remembered for their courage in signing the Delaration, an act considered treasonous by the British Crown.
